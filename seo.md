PART 1 — How to Internally Link BACK to the Pillar (Critical for SEO)

If /debt-payoff is your main pillar, then:

Every related blog post must link back to it.

Not randomly.
Not in the footer.
Not 10 times.

✅ The Correct Way

Each blog article should include:

One contextual link near the top

One strategic CTA section near the bottom




1️⃣ Contextual Link (Near the Top — Within First 2–3 Paragraphs)

Example inside /blog/minimum-payment-trap:

<p>
  If you're serious about eliminating debt permanently, start with a
  structured plan. This complete
  <Link
    href="/debt-payoff"
    className="text-[#4472C4] font-semibold hover:underline"
  >
    debt payoff guide
  </Link>
  breaks down the exact system step-by-step.
</p>

Why near the top?

Because Google gives more weight to early internal links.

This tells Google:

“/debt-payoff is the central authority page.”

2️⃣ Structured CTA Section (Near Bottom of Article)

Add this before the closing wrapper div of each blog post:

<section className="mt-16 bg-[#1F4E78] text-white rounded-xl p-8 text-center">
  <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold mb-4">
    Ready to Eliminate Your Debt?
  </h3>
  <p className="mb-6 opacity-90">
    See the complete step-by-step system for choosing Snowball or Avalanche,
    calculating timelines, and accelerating payoff.
  </p>

  <Link
    href="/debt-payoff"
    className="inline-block bg-white text-[#1F4E78] px-6 py-3 rounded-full font-bold hover:scale-105 transition"
  >
    View the Complete Debt Payoff Guide →
  </Link>
</section>

Now every article:

Sends authority back to the pillar

Funnels traffic

Reinforces topical relevance

That’s how clusters work.
