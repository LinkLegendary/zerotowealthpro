# About the ZeroToWealthPro Editorial Team

ZeroToWealthPro publishes personal finance guides focused on one problem: how real households get out of debt, build financial stability, and stop the paycheck-to-paycheck cycle — without financial jargon, unrealistic assumptions, or advice calibrated for people who already have money.

## What We Cover

Every article on this site falls into one of four categories:

**Debt strategy** — payoff sequencing, method comparisons (snowball vs avalanche), balance transfers, interest rate negotiation, payday loan exits, collections strategy, and credit rebuilding after debt.

**Math and traps** — the actual cost of minimum payments, the true APR on common debt products, month-by-month payoff timelines, and the specific numbers behind decisions most people make without calculating.

**Tools and habits** — budgeting apps, payoff calculators, behavior change frameworks, and the structural habits that determine whether a plan survives contact with a bad month.

**Budgeting for tight incomes** — systems designed specifically for households where the standard 50/30/20 advice does not apply because fixed expenses consume 85–95% of take-home income.

## Our Editorial Standards

**Every article is built around real numbers.** We do not publish percentage-based advice without calculating what that percentage means in dollars for a specific household at a specific income level.

**Every external claim is sourced.** We cite the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, the Federal Trade Commission, the National Foundation for Credit Counseling, peer-reviewed journals, and major financial publications. We link to the primary source, not a secondary summary of it.

**We do not publish content we cannot verify.** If a statistic cannot be traced to a primary source with a live URL, it does not appear in our articles.

**We flag dead links.** URLs rot. We review external links regularly and replace dead citations with verified live alternatives rather than leaving broken references in published articles.

**We do not accept payment for editorial coverage.** Our articles do not recommend specific financial products in exchange for compensation. Where affiliate relationships exist, they are disclosed. Advertisers do not influence editorial content.

## How Our Articles Are Written

Each article is produced through a structured research and writing process:

1. Primary sources identified and reviewed before writing begins
2. Key claims verified against the cited source
3. Math examples calculated and cross-checked
4. Internal links verified as live before publication
5. External links verified as live before publication
6. Article reviewed against our editorial checklist before publishing

Articles are updated when source URLs change, when underlying data is revised by the issuing organization, or when reader feedback identifies an error.

## Contact and Corrections

If you find an error — a dead link, an incorrect statistic, a calculation that does not match the cited source — please use the contact form on this site. We correct errors promptly and update the article's `lastUpdated` date when substantive changes are made.
